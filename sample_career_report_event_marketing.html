Profile Assessment

Jordan sits at a genuinely interesting inflection point in event marketing. Ten years of consistent progression — from Events Associate through to Director of Global Events at large publicly-traded B2C and DTC companies — represents a track record that is both rare and highly legible to hiring managers. The trajectory tells a clear story of operational depth that grew into strategic scope: somebody who learned the trade from the ground up and now owns the program.

The combination of trade show mastery, global brand activation experience, and budget ownership at the Director level is a strong differentiator in a market where most mid-career event professionals have depth in one area but not all three. Add CMP certification — which PCMA data shows correlates with roughly $6,000–$11,000 higher average compensation than uncertified peers — and Jordan carries the credential stack that large corporate event teams look for when hiring managers and senior individual contributors.

The primary area to manage carefully is team scale. A maximum team of three direct reports, none of whom managed others, means Jordan's positioning as a "director" is accurate by title but may be questioned in interviews for VP-level or larger director roles where people management scope is a gatekeeping criterion. Reframing toward program scale, vendor portfolio complexity, and budget owned — rather than headcount managed — is the right narrative strategy.

Gaps & Opportunities

Gaps — Things Costing Jordan Right Now

Credential Gap
No Digital or Hybrid Event Credential

Jordan's CMP is strong, but the absence of any hybrid/virtual event certification reads as a gap to hiring managers in 2026. The field moved permanently toward hybrid after 2020, and Director-level candidates are now expected to demonstrate formal literacy in digital event strategy — not just in-person execution. DES closes this gap directly and carries a documented salary premium on top of CMP.

Positioning Gap
Team Size May Stall VP-Level Progression

Managing three direct reports, none with their own reports, is below the threshold many VP-level job descriptions specify. This isn't a reflection of Jordan's competence — it reflects the organizational structure of previous employers. The fix is intentional: either seek roles that offer team growth, or frame scope in terms of vendor relationships managed, budget owned, and cross-functional stakeholders influenced rather than headcount supervised.

Measurement Gap
No Documented ROI Narrative

The event marketing market is moving sharply toward outcome measurement — dwell time, lead conversion, post-event sales lift, brand sentiment change. If Jordan cannot speak fluently to how past activations moved business metrics, it creates a competency gap with hiring managers who have been burned by event teams that operated as cost centers rather than growth drivers. GA4 certification and one documented case study solve this immediately.

Visibility Gap
Low Professional Visibility Outside Current Employer

Ten years of strong execution at large companies may have been done primarily in the background. IS types often prefer to let the work speak for itself, but at Director level, external visibility — through LinkedIn posts, industry panels, or MPI/PCMA chapter participation — is how peers and recruiters know Jordan exists. The event industry is relationship-dense; the fastest path to new opportunities is being known before a role opens up, not after.
